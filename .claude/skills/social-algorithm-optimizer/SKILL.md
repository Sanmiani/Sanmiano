# Social Algorithm Optimizer

Analyzes and optimizes social media posts for maximum reach using platform algorithm insights. Applies ranking signal principles to rewrite and improve content for Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, and Twitter/X.

## When to Use This Skill

- Optimizing a post draft before publishing
- Understanding why a post underperformed
- Rewriting content to align with platform ranking signals
- Building audience in a specific niche
- Debugging inconsistent engagement

## How Platform Algorithms Work

### Core Ranking Signals (apply across all platforms)

**Real engagement prediction**: Will your specific followers engage with this content?
- Direct questions get more replies than statements
- Community-specific language resonates within niches
- Relatable vulnerability builds connection

**Community resonance**: Does this fit within a recognized interest cluster?
- Picking ONE clear topic per post beats mixed messages
- Using community language (shared terminology, inside references)
- Staying in your niche strengthens your topical identity

**Content-creator fit**: Does this match your established identity?
- Consistent topics help algorithms categorize you correctly
- Off-brand pivots confuse recommendation systems
- Topic authority compounds over time

**Engagement signal weighting** (high to low):
1. Saves/bookmarks — highest signal (user wants to return)
2. Shares/retweets — strong (user puts reputation behind it)
3. Comments/replies — engagement quality signal
4. Likes — basic positive signal
5. Time spent — implicit quality signal

**Negative signals** (avoid):
- Content that gets reported or hidden
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree") — hurts credibility long-term
- Generic statements with no specific audience

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Instagram (Reels and Feed)
- Hook in first 0–3 seconds is critical for Reels
- Saves matter more than likes for feed posts
- Captions that ask questions drive comments
- Consistency in posting days/times signals reliability
- Hashtags: 3–5 highly relevant > 30 generic

### LinkedIn
- Longer posts with line breaks outperform short ones
- Personal stories with professional lessons perform best
- Tag people who are genuinely relevant (not for reach)
- First comment from yourself (with your link) keeps reach clean
- Best days: Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9 AM or 12–1 PM local

### Facebook (Page)
- Video native uploads outperform YouTube links
- Posts that spark comments beat posts that get likes
- Ask a direct question at the end
- Avoid external links in the post body (use first comment)
- Community framing ("brothers", "we", "our community") drives shares

### Twitter/X
- Reply to replies quickly — active conversations get boosted
- Threads often outperform single tweets
- First hour of engagement is critical
- End with a direct question to trigger replies

## Optimization Process

### Step 1: Identify the Core Message
- What is the single most important thing this post communicates?
- Who specifically should care about this?
- What engagement do you want? (saves, comments, shares)

### Step 2: Map to Algorithm Strategy
- Which follower segment will engage?
- Which niche community does this serve?
- Does this fit your established identity?

### Step 3: Optimize for Signals

**To trigger saves**: Tutorials, how-tos, data/stats, resources people will reference
**To trigger shares**: Information that makes the sharer look good, representational value
**To trigger comments**: Direct questions, debates, incomplete thoughts that invite completion
**To trigger likes**: Novel insights, validation of beliefs, strong opinions with evidence

### Step 4: Check Against Negatives
- Any risk of being reported or hidden?
- Any confusion about your content identity?
- Any engagement bait language?

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Generic statements | Add a specific, unexpected angle |
| No call to action | End with a direct question |
| Too long for platform | Trim to platform norm; use line breaks |
| No hook | Lead with the most surprising/useful line |
| Off-brand topic | Stay in your niche or clearly signal the shift |
| External link in body | Move link to first comment (Facebook/LinkedIn) |
| Pure engagement bait | Earn engagement through genuine value |

## Quality Principle

Consistent engagement from your community beats occasional viral moments. Algorithm optimization is not about tricks — it's about making genuinely valuable content easy for the algorithm to surface to the right people.
