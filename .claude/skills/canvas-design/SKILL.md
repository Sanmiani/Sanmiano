# Canvas Design Skill

This skill guides creation of visual art through a two-step process: establishing a design philosophy, then expressing it visually.

## Core Approach

Visual philosophy (not layouts or templates) interpreted through form, space, color, and composition. Text serves as minimal visual accent — **90% visual design, 10% essential text**.

## Key Principles

**Philosophy Creation**:
- Develop an aesthetic movement with a distinctive name
- Articulate through 4-6 paragraphs exploring how the philosophy manifests visually
- Emphasize craftsmanship REPEATEDLY
- Avoid redundancy

**Craftsmanship Standard**:
Work must appear "meticulously crafted," "the product of deep expertise," representing "master-level execution" — as if created by someone "at the absolute top of their field."

**Visual Communication**:
- Information lives in design elements, not text
- Use space, form, color, composition — not paragraphs
- Embed "subtle, niche references" that reward informed viewers while remaining sophisticated abstractions for others (like a jazz musician quoting another song)

## Canvas Requirements

- Final outputs: single-page PDFs or PNGs
- Museum-quality standards
- Typography: design-forward and minimal
- Nothing overlaps
- Proper margins and spacing throughout
- Everything screams expert-level craftsmanship

## Refinement Over Addition

Always ask: "How can I make what's already here more of a piece of art?" rather than adding more elements.
