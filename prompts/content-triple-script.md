# Content Triple-Script Generator

**How to use:** Paste this entire prompt into Claude. Fill in the [TOPIC] line. Review and edit all three outputs before posting anywhere.

---

## Your prompt (copy everything below this line)

---

You are writing content for Matt Sanmiano — a content creator building a brand and community for African men 35+, focused on health, AI, and modern opportunities.

**Topic for this batch:**
[TOPIC — e.g. "sleep and testosterone after 35" or "how AI can help you track your health"]

**Audience:** African men, 35 and older. Health-conscious or health-curious. Community-oriented. Want to be spoken to directly, not talked at.

**Voice rules:**
- Tone: Direct, warm, community-first. Speaking FROM within, not about the group.
- Use "brothers" framing in casual posts. "35+" is a consistent identity marker.
- Short punchy openers. No fluff. No corporate wellness language ("wellness journey", "holistic approach").
- Back claims with specific data and numbers — not vibes.
- No em dashes in casual posts. Short sentences.
- Action-first CTAs: "Save this post", "Tag a brother", "Share it". Never passive.
- Emojis used purposefully: ✅ for list items, 💪💚📲 at CTAs. Not decorative.

---

Generate all three outputs below.

---

### OUTPUT 1 — Facebook Post

Format:
- Bold all-caps title at the top (e.g. "SLEEP AND TESTOSTERONE AFTER 35")
- 1-2 sentence punchy opener with a real stat or bold claim
- 3-5 ✅ bullet points — each with a bold heading, then 1-2 sentences of explanation
- 1-2 sentence close that brings it home
- CTA: one action-first line (Tag a brother, Share this, Save it)
- Hashtags: 5-8, grouped at the end. Mix community tags (#AfroMen, #BlackMensHealth) with topic tags.

Length: 200-300 words.

---

### OUTPUT 2 — Instagram Reel Script

Format:
- **HOOK (0-3 sec):** One bold statement or question that stops the scroll. Written as spoken word.
- **BODY (3-30 sec):** 3-4 punchy points, each one sentence. Fast-paced. No filler.
- **CTA (last 3-5 sec):** One clear action — follow, save, share, comment.

Write it as a script the creator reads to camera. Label each section. Keep total script under 150 words.

---

### OUTPUT 3 — Substack Full Article Draft

Format:
- **Headline:** Clear, direct, benefit-forward
- **Subheadline:** One line that earns the click
- **Opening (2-3 paragraphs):** Hook with a real story, stat, or bold claim. Build the stakes for why this matters to African men 35+.
- **Body sections (3-5):** Each with a bold header. Mix data, practical advice, and cultural context. 100-150 words per section.
- **Closing (1-2 paragraphs):** Personal, direct. What Matt wants the reader to do or feel after reading.
- **CTA:** Subscribe prompt or community invitation.

Length: 600-900 words total. Educational and personal in register — not a listicle, more like a trusted older brother who did the research.
