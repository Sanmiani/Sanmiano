## Sample 2 — Heart health post (long-form, educational)

```
HEART HEALTH FOR MEN 35+

Your heart works overtime every single day. At 35+, it's time to give it the respect it deserves.

Heart disease is the #1 killer of African men — but it doesn't have to be your story.

our heart pumps about 2,000 gallons of blood daily. It's the hardest working muscle in your body, and African men face a 30% higher risk of heart disease than other groups.
Here's how to protect your engine:

✅ KNOW YOUR RISK NUMBERS — Get your blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar checked annually. Target BP under 130/80, LDL cholesterol under 100. These numbers tell your heart's story before symptoms appear.
✅ EAT THE RAINBOW — Load up on leafy greens, okra, black-eyed peas, and salmon. These foods fight inflammation and keep arteries clear. Cut the salt shaker and limit processed foods — your heart feels the difference within weeks.
✅ MOVE LIKE YOUR LIFE DEPENDS ON IT — Because it does. 150 minutes of moderate exercise weekly cuts heart disease risk in half. Brisk walks, dancing, swimming — pick something you enjoy and do it consistently.
✅ MANAGE STRESS LIKE A SKILL — Chronic stress raises cortisol, which damages your cardiovascular system over time. Meditation, deep breathing, or just turning off the news — find your reset button and use it daily.
✅ SLEEP IS NOT OPTIONAL — 7-8 hours nightly allows your heart to rest and repair. Sleep apnea is common in Black men and strains the heart — talk to your doctor if you snore loudly or wake gasping.

💚 Tag a brother who needs to prioritize his heart health.
📲 Share this post — it could save a life.

#HeartHealth #BlackMensHealth #CardiovascularHealth #MensWellness #AfroMen #HeartDiseasePrevention #HealthyLiving
```

## Register notes

- **Tone:** Direct, warm, community-first. Speaks TO African men, not about them.
- **Framing:** "Brothers" not "Black men" in casual usage. "35+" is a consistent identity marker.
- **Structure:** Checklist format with ✅ bullets. Bold all-caps headers for sections. Short punchy openers.
- **CTA style:** Action-first — "Save this post", "Tag a brother", "Share it". Never passive.
- **Data use:** Specific numbers (4,700mg, 30% higher risk, 150 minutes) — backs claims with facts, not vibes.
- **No em dashes in casual posts.** Uses "—" sparingly. Short sentences. No fluff.
- **Hashtags:** Grouped at the end on longer posts. Community-specific (#AfroMen, #BlackMensHealth).
- **Emojis:** Used purposefully — ✅ for lists, 💪💚📲 at CTAs. Not decorative.

## What NOT to do with this voice

- Don't write in third person about "African men" as a group he's observing — he's speaking from within.
- Don't use corporate wellness language ("wellness journey", "holistic approach").
- Don't fake urgency with clickbait. His urgency is real and backed by data.
- Don't generate LinkedIn content that sounds like Instagram — LinkedIn posts are more reflective and professional in register, less checklist-heavy.
