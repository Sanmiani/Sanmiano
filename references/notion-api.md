# Notion MCP — Reference Guide

**Status:** Connected via claude.ai MCP
**What it unlocks:** Read, create, and update Notion pages and databases — your knowledge base and content tracker.

---

## What you can do

| Action | How to ask |
|---|---|
| Search Notion | "Find any health research notes in my Notion" |
| Read a page | "Show me my content ideas database" |
| Create a page | "Create a Notion page for my weekly content plan" |
| Update a page | "Add this caption idea to my Notion content tracker" |
| Create a database | "Create a Notion database to track my posts across platforms" |
| Get comments | "Any comments or notes on my Notion pages?" |

---

## Best use cases for your content workflow

1. **Content database** — Build a table tracking every post: platform, topic, date, status, link, performance
2. **Idea inbox** — A simple Notion page where content ideas get dropped and sorted weekly
3. **Health research wiki** — Summaries of health stats and studies to pull from when writing
4. **SOPs** — Document your posting process so the AIOS can follow it

---

## Example prompts

```
Create a Notion database called "Content Tracker" with these fields:
- Title (text)
- Platform (select: Instagram / LinkedIn / Facebook)
- Topic (text)
- Post Date (date)
- Status (select: Idea / Draft / Scheduled / Posted)
- Link (URL)
- Notes (text)
```

```
Create a Notion page called "Health Research Bank" with sections for:
Blood Pressure, Heart Health, Nutrition, Fitness, Mental Health, Sleep.
I'll add research notes to each section over time.
```

```
Search my Notion for any content ideas or health notes I've already saved.
```

---

## Notes

- Notion MCP is powerful for building your "second brain" — health research, content ideas, post archive
- Start with two things: a Content Tracker database and a Health Research Bank page
- Once your Notion structure is set, Claude can drop ideas directly into your tracker as you work
