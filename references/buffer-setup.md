# Buffer Setup Guide

Buffer is a free social media scheduler. You connect your accounts once, then schedule posts in one place instead of logging into each platform separately.

---

## Step 1 — Create your Buffer account

1. Go to buffer.com
2. Sign up with your Google account (mattsanmiano@gmail.com)
3. Choose the **Free plan** — it covers 3 channels and 10 scheduled posts at a time. Enough for now.

---

## Step 2 — Connect your three channels

In Buffer, go to **Settings → Channels → Connect a Channel**.

Connect in this order:

### Facebook Page
1. Click **Facebook Page**
2. Log in to Facebook when prompted
3. Select your **Strongafter35afrimen** Page (not your personal profile)
4. Approve permissions

### Instagram (Business account required)
1. Click **Instagram**
2. Buffer connects Instagram via Facebook — you'll need your Instagram linked to your Facebook Page
3. If your Instagram isn't a Business account yet: go to Instagram → Settings → Account → Switch to Professional Account → Creator or Business
4. Once it's a Business account, connect it through Buffer using your Facebook login
5. Select the correct Instagram account

### LinkedIn
1. Click **LinkedIn**
2. Log in with your LinkedIn credentials
3. Choose **Personal Profile** (you can add a Company Page later if you create one)
4. Approve permissions

**Verify:** All three channels should show a green "Connected" status on your Buffer dashboard.

---

## Step 3 — Set your posting schedule

In Buffer, go to each channel → **Posting Schedule**.

Set these time slots (you can adjust based on your analytics later):

| Channel | Day | Time |
|---------|-----|------|
| Facebook | Tuesday | 7:00 PM |
| Instagram | Thursday | 7:00 PM |
| LinkedIn | Saturday | 9:00 AM |

Buffer will auto-fill these slots when you queue a post.

---

## Step 4 — Your weekly workflow

Once Buffer is set up, your weekly posting workflow is:

1. **Run triple-script prompt** → get Facebook post + Reel script + Substack draft
2. **Run posting-pack prompt** → get Instagram caption + LinkedIn post + schedule + Buffer checklist
3. **Open Buffer** → follow the Buffer Paste Guide (OUTPUT D from the posting-pack)
4. **Review in Buffer calendar** → confirm all three posts are queued
5. **Done.** Buffer posts automatically at the scheduled times.

Total time per week: ~20-30 minutes for content review and scheduling (down from logging in three times and writing per platform).
